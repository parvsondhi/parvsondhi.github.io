MOTIVATION

The aim of the project was to model and build a functioning Prison Database Management System. The prison systems in India were very primitive and did not support easy user interactions. As part of this project we focussed on designing the backend architecture in terms of the database that would be required to maximize the utility of such an online system.

Note: The purpose of the project was to understand the process of designing and modelling the database and hence, this project did not include front end design

Key Concepts: Entity Relationship - Modelling, UML Diagramming, Lo-fi Prototyping, Information Organization, Database Design
Tools and Langauages: MySQL, DML scripting

Simple Entity Relationship Structure

Based on our model, we srarted with a very simple entity relationship diagram, to highlight the basic structure that we would work on. This model served as our basis for further development.

ER Diagram - Iteration 2

With the basic structure ready, we realised that for the system to be usable, we needed to model many more interactions from the perspective of the potential users of the system. We started to work on expanding the model to include different entities involved. The structure started to take shape of Multi Method complex relationship model. With this diagram, we started to realise the classes that would be involved and the relationships they would hold with each other.

Final Design

ER Diagram

We designed the complete structure of the database to support multiple interactions. The final ER diagram modelled the various entities and thier properties. We designed multiple features, and modified our database to support them. The list of features supported by the database include, but not limited to, are:

  • Only the correct authorities can start the project
  • After logging in, a home page opens in which details about prisoners, jail authority figures, about prison etc.Â 
  • All information is processed into the prison database every time a prisoner is accepted into the prison.
  • Each prisoner is related to its unique prison id at the prison. The prison database contains all information of about each and every prisoner.
  • Information related to the prison guards, including personal information and also the cells they are guarding ,etc are also available for viewing by authorities.
  • The prison is consisting of various types of cells/jails which hold a maximum of 4 and a minimum of 2 prisoners.
  • Each prisoner is given a basic violence rating which determines how much the prisoner is prone to violence.
  • Along with violence rating a prison guard can also award points of merit for good behaviour to the prisoner which may lead to an early release from prison
  • Various wards present at the prison such as Solitary Confinement, general population, psychiatric ward ,etc are along with their basic information such as number of prisoners, etc are also stored
  • Prisoners are also divided on the basis of their sentence duration and hence their date of parole is stored.
  • Any relative of the prisoner can view basic information about the prisoner and also fix an appointment to meet him/her during their conviction.
